About

Researching reliable and efficient machine learning systems

I am interested in building reliable, efficient, and scientifically useful machine learning systems. My research has explored black-box uncertainty quantification for LLM-based evaluation, efficiency methods such as pruning and distillation, and machine learning applications ranging from quantum models to scientific combinatorics.

Through multiple research and engineering internships at IBM, including work with the MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab, I have contributed to projects at the intersection of modern foundation models, evaluation, and AI for science.
